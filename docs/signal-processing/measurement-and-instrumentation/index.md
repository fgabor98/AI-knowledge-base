# Measurement And Instrumentation

## Taxonomy

- Parent: [Signal Processing](../index.md)
- Sibling subtopics: [Signals And Systems](../signals-and-systems/index.md), [Digital Signal Processing](../digital-signal-processing/index.md), [Infocommunication](../infocommunication/index.md)
- Page type: subtopic index
- Theory page: [Theory](theory.md)

- Primary role: practical acquisition, calibration, synchronization, and validation of measured signals

## Scope

Measurement And Instrumentation owns the signal path from physical process to recorded data: sensors, signal conditioning, ADC/DAC behavior, oscilloscopes, measurement records, uncertainty, timing, distributed synchronization, calibration, compensation, and lab validation workflows.

## Learning Order

1. Measurement-chain model
2. Time-domain measurement
3. Frequency-domain measurement
4. Transfer-characteristic measurement
5. Measurement uncertainty and calibration traceability
6. Compensation and calibration
7. Distributed measurements and synchronization
8. Real-time lab workflows

## Page Responsibilities

- [Theory](theory.md): measurement concepts, error sources, timing models, synchronization, and compensation principles.
- [Programming](programming.md): trace parsing, metric extraction, FFT analysis, calibration scripts, synchronization correction, and lab-data validation.
- [References](references.md): source PDFs, extraction confidence, and pending manual checks.
